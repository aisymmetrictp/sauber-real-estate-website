Common Questions

Buyer FAQs

How much do I need for a down payment? +

Down payments vary by loan type. Conventional loans typically require 3-20%, FHA loans as low as 3.5%, and VA/USDA loans may require 0% down. Minnesota also has first-time buyer assistance programs that can help with down payment and closing costs. I can connect you with trusted lenders who specialize in these programs.

How long does it take to buy a home in Minnesota? +

The typical timeline from offer acceptance to closing is 30-45 days. However, getting pre-approved and finding the right home can vary. In the current market, well-prepared buyers often find homes within 2-8 weeks of starting their search. Being pre-approved before you start looking puts you in the strongest position.

Do I need a real estate agent to buy a home? +

While not legally required, having a buyer's agent is one of the smartest moves you can make. I provide access to the full MLS, negotiate on your behalf, coordinate inspections and appraisals, and guide you through the paperwork — all at no cost to you as the buyer in most transactions.

What are the best neighborhoods for families? +

Minnesota's south metro has incredible family-friendly communities. Apple Valley, Lakeville, and Eagan consistently rank among the best for schools, parks, and safety. For more space and a rural feel, Farmington and Rosemount offer great value. I'll help match your priorities — schools, commute, lifestyle — to the perfect neighborhood.
